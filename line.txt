NFT, or Non-fungible Tokens, are a type of cryptocurrency that is unique and cannot be replaced by another token of the same type. This is in contrast to "fungible" tokens, which are interchangeable and can be replaced by another token of the same type.

NFTs are created when a blockchain user "burns" or irrevocably destroys a certain number of tokens. This creates a unique and permanent record on the blockchain of the number of NFTs that exist.

NFTs can be used to represent a variety of assets, such as digital artwork, domain names, or gaming assets. They can also be used to represent rights and privileges, such as voting rights or access to a certain service.

NFTs are often used to create digital collectibles, which are items that can be collected and traded online. These collectibles are created by combining a unique piece of digital artwork with a NFT that represents the ownership of that artwork.
